I paint emotions
before I name them.

My work is rooted in emotion — raw, layered, sometimes chaotic, sometimes full of light. Using watercolor and mixed media on 450g cotton paper, I give shape to the unspoken: fragile beauty, sorrow, and contradictions of being human.

I experience the world with heightened sensitivity — even a form of synesthesia. Music, light, and sensory fragments become color, texture, and atmosphere. My paintings invite you to feel, not decode, each piece: fluid, shifting, uncertain, alive.

Light is a recurring thread in my work.
